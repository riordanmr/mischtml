Slide Show Research

Here's the result of my research on free tools to create slideshow videos from photos, with music in the background. There are a lot more ways to do this; these are just the ones I found before deciding to bring my research to a close. It so happened that all the tools I reviewed were web-based.

The tools are rated roughly in decreasing order of preference, based on my perception of what the average user would prefer.

For each tool, I created a sample slideshow from the same set of photos.

Media.io (by WonderShare)

Media.io offers many photo and video tools; the photos-to-slideshow tool seems to be one of their more obscure ones. I put this tool at the top of the list mostly because it's the easiest to use.

Pros:

  • Easy to use
  • Free version seems to have no limitions like watermarking of output, etc.

Cons:

  • Extremely basic; very few options. For instance, there's no way to add text to your slides, or to tweak your photos after uploading.
  • There doesn't seem to be a way to save projects from one session to the next, even though the website implies otherwise.
  • The website is poorly laid out. I stumbled upon the slideshow maker at https://www.media.io/slideshow-maker.html and had to make a bookmark because it's difficult to find on the website.

Adobe Express

Adobe Express is the free version of many of Adobe's tools. Adobe is known for its expensive, hard-to-learn, professional-grade applications, and Adobe Express reflects that heritage, even though it's free and somewhat dumbed-down.

Pros:

  • Quite powerful, especially for a free tool.
  • Free version seems to have few limitions like watermarking of output, etc.
  • Unlike many of its competitors, Adobe is a well-established company that is likely to be around for a long time.

Cons:

  • There's a significant learning curve.
  • You have to do a lot of work to get a slideshow that looks halfway decent. The tool is more interested in allowing you to create exactly what you want than in making it easy to create anything.

Animoto

Animoto is a website that is largely oriented towards making slideshows. It would be my top recommendation if it weren't for the fact that it watermarks the output of its free version.

Pros:

  • Fairly powerful; approaches Adobe Express in that regard.
  • Easy to use.

Cons:

  • Free version leaves a watermark that is present on the entire video.

Kapwing

Kapwing is a fairly powerful video editing website that approaches Adobe Express in slideshow-relevant capabilities, while being perhaps a bit easier to use.

Pros:

  • Fairly powerful.

Cons:

  • They really, really want you to get a paid account. In fact, you can't get a free account at all, though you can use the website without an account. I'm not sure how it tracks your projects from one session to the next.
  • Free version does not allow export of videos, so the only way you could share them is to send or post a link to your project on the kapwing.com website.
