1960s Trentman / Riordan Recordings

Below are links to recordings made by the Jack and Mary Riordan family and the Don and Eileen Trentman family, on reel-to-reel tape recorders in the 1960s. They sent tapes back and forth between the US and Japan, as this was less expensive than international long-distance phone calls at the time.

The recordings can be difficult to follow. Although the technical quality is mostly good, tapes were often partly overwritten, with confusing results. A given tape might start with one group of people, jump to another time with another group of people, then revert back to the first group - often with some inexplicable musical interludes.

These recordings were transcribed to compact disc by a friend perhaps around 2010, then later ripped to MP3.
